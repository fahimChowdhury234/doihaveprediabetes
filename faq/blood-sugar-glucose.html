What is blood sugar?

  • Blood sugar serves as your body's primary source of energy and is derived from the food you consume
  • The main sugar present in your blood is glucose, which is transported by your bloodstream to all cells in your body for energy utilization.
  • However, prolonged elevated levels of glucose in your blood can lead to significant health issues over time.

What is the normal range for blood sugar?

  • A normal level for an A1C test is below 5.7.

What is the normal level for blood sugar in a blood glucose test?

  • Normal levels for a fasting blood glucose test are 99 mg/dL or below.

Are there symptoms of high blood sugar?

  • Signs of high blood sugar may not always be evident, underscoring the importance of discussing your risk for type 2 diabetes with your doctor
  • Individuals with elevated blood sugar levels may experience symptoms such as blurry vision, increased thirst, fatigue, and frequent urination. However, relying solely on symptoms is not a reliable indicator of whether one has type 2 diabetes. It is essential to consult with your doctor for accurate assessment and guidance.

What are the effects of high blood sugar?

  • Prolonged high blood sugar levels can lead to severe health complications, including heart disease, vision loss, and kidney disease
  • In the case of diabetes, the body faces challenges either in producing sufficient insulin or utilizing the insulin it pr oduces effectively
  • Insulin plays a crucial role in regulating the amount of glucose in your bloodstream at any given time. When there is an insufficient amount of insulin, it results in elevated blood sugar levels

How can you lower your blood sugar?

  • You can lower your blood sugar (and risk for prediabetes and type 2 diabetes) by making healthy lifestyle changes. These include losing weight, eating healthier, and getting regular physical activity. The CDC - led National Diabetes Prevention Program lifestyle change program can help you make healthy changes that last
